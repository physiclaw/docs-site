This page is a **map, not the territory**. The authoritative, bolt-by-bolt instructions live in
the build manual; this page exists to put the manual in order in your head, warn you about the
four or five steps that silently break everything if you get them wrong, and tell you what to do
after the last bolt is in.

Before you turn a single bolt: read the whole manual end to end, and re-check the complete
[bill of materials](/en/hardware/bill-of-materials/). This is a from-scratch, hand-built **CoreXY
gantry** — extrusion, rails, belts, and printed joints — not a ready-made plotter with a pen to
swap. Budget **about two to three hours** for assembly itself (the manual's own figure), sitting
inside a multi-day project: source/print/cut, then assemble, then flash firmware, then calibrate
and tune.

:::note[The manual is the real instructions]
The authoritative build manual is **13 JSON sections**, in this order:
`00_front`, `01_frame`, `02_idler`, `03_motor`, `04_linear`, `05_belt`, `06_stylus`, `07_phone`,
`08_pcb`, `09_camera`, `10_wiring`, `11_bom`, `99_back`. It is backed by **~71 build123d procedure
modules** under `hardware/assembly/procedures/`. Follow that manual bolt-by-bolt. This page only
orients and warns.
:::

## The stage order, and why each comes first

Assembly is a dependency chain. Each stage builds on the one before it, so the order is not
optional — doing the belt before the rails, or the camera before the frame is square, forces you
to take work apart.

<Steps>

1. **Fastener (reference only).** Not a build stage — the `00_front` section is your key to the
   fastener types and T-nuts used throughout (drop-in vs slide-in T-nuts, BHCS/FHCS/SHCS sizes).
   Read it so the later sections make sense.

2. **Frame (`01_frame`).** Build the box: 4x 2040 black-anodized extrusion — 2x **345 mm** side
   rails (counterbored M6) and 2x **190 mm** end rails (tapped M6) — plus 1x 1020 **185 mm**
   gantry X-beam and 2x 1020 **230 mm** phone-bed supports. **Square the box against a known-flat
   surface before anything else hangs off it.** Out-of-square here propagates into every later
   stage — the rails will bind, the belt tension will never equalize, the screen will not sit flat
   under the camera.

3. **Idler (`02_idler`).** Mount the idler stacks to their printed idler mounts. These define the
   belt path, so they go on before the belt.

4. **Motor (`03_motor`).** Bolt the 2x NEMA 17 steppers to their motor brackets. They stay fixed —
   in CoreXY the motors do not move with the carriage.

5. **Linear (`04_linear`).** Mount the 3x MGN9H rails (2x **240 mm** Y, 1x **150 mm** X) and their
   3x H-type carriages (4x M3 each, **16x15 mm** mounting pitch), then the printed XY joints (left
   and right). Take your time aligning the rails — the manual says "be patient" here for a reason.

6. **Belt (`05_belt`).** Cut the GT2 6 mm belt from the 5 m roll into equal A and B lengths, fit
   the 2x GT2 20T drive pulleys on the stepper shafts, and route through all 12 idlers (10 smooth
   + 2 toothed). The CoreXY routing must be correct and A/B must end up at **equal tension**.

7. **Stylus / solenoid — "tapz" (`06_stylus`).** Mount the solenoid, do the Z-conversion, and set
   the stylus tip geometry. This is the stage the old docs omitted entirely — see the dedicated
   section below.

8. **Phone bed (`07_phone`).** Seat the phone in its printed bed with its **top-left corner
   registered to a fixed corner** of the bed.

9. **Board / PCB (`08_pcb`).** Seat the MKS DLC32 V2.1 board in its printed control-board holder.

10. **Camera (`09_camera`).** Mount the 4K/8MP autofocus USB webcam on its printed mount, looking
    straight down at the screen.

11. **Wiring (`10_wiring`).** Wire it all up — last, so nothing tugs while you position parts. Yes,
    there is soldering.

</Steps>

## The steps that silently brick the rig

A perfectly-built frame can still do nothing if one of these is wrong. None of them throw an
error — they just fail quietly. Front-load your attention here.

### Square the frame first

Covered above, but it bears repeating as a gotcha: an out-of-square box is the root cause of
binding rails and uneven belt tension downstream. Square it on a flat reference surface in stage 2,
and verify it before moving on.

### Re-grease the carriages — do not run them dry

The MGN9H carriages **ship coated in factory shipping oil**, which is a preservative, not a
lubricant. Wipe it off and re-grease with **lithium grease (NLGI 2)** before mounting. Running them
dry wears the balls and adds friction the steppers have to fight.

### Loctite the set screws — the #1 reported failure

:::caution[Loose drive-pulley set screws are the single most common failure]
Apply **Loctite 243** to set screws — especially the drive-pulley set screws on the stepper shafts
— and generally to any fastener the manual flags. A loose drive-pulley set screw lets the pulley
slip on the shaft: the arm thinks it moved, the carriage didn't, and every position drifts.
Thread-lock is not optional here.
:::

CoreXY also depends on **equal A/B belt tension**, with a tension target measured on the next page.
Getting the routing and tension right is a distinct phase — see [tuning](/en/hardware/tuning/) for
the belt-tension target (measured with a phone tuning app) and the rail-alignment routine. Don't
restate the number here; tune it there.

### Stylus earth-grounding — the silent "do-nothing" failure

:::caution[The capacitive tip MUST be tied to a verified earth ground]
The capacitive stylus only registers as a finger if it is connected to a **verified earth ground**
— for example, a grounded PC chassis. **Never** the wall socket directly, and **never** the
control-board GND. Without earth grounding, the taps will not register on the phone **at all**: you
get a flawlessly-built rig that touches the glass and the phone ignores it. This is also a safety
item — see [safety](/en/safety/).
:::

### Camera and phone-corner rigidity — silent re-calibration

The camera reads on-screen positions from its image, and calibration learns the mapping from screen
to arm. **Both depend on nothing moving after you calibrate.** Mount the webcam rigidly on its
printed mount so it cannot flex or get bumped, frame the whole screen with margin, and seat the
phone so its top-left corner returns to the exact same spot every session. A bumped camera or a
reseated-slightly-off phone moves the screen out from under the positions the arm learned and forces
a re-[calibration](/en/set-up/calibrate/).

## The Z stage in detail: solenoid + stylus (tapz)

This is the stage most likely to be new to you, because there is no pen and no servo. The Z/touch
actuator is a **12 V 0.8 A push-pull solenoid** (10 mm stroke, 0.2–6 N force, 2x M3 mounts at
**15 mm** pitch). The **capacitive stylus tip** (M3 female thread, ~Ø7 mm conductive head) threads
onto the solenoid **core** — in place of the nut the solenoid ships with.

<Steps>

1. **Mount the solenoid** to its printed solenoid mount (2x M3 at 15 mm pitch), on the X carriage.

2. **Do the Z-conversion.** Remove the nut shipped on the solenoid core and thread the capacitive
   stylus tip on in its place. The stylus is now the moving Z end.

3. **Set the tip geometry** so the tip touches the glass **flat**, not at an angle, when the
   solenoid fires — and clears the glass when released.

4. **Earth-ground the tip** (see the caution above — this is what makes the taps register).

</Steps>

{/* PHOTO: close-up of the solenoid on its printed mount on the X carriage, the capacitive stylus
     tip threaded onto the core where the nut used to be, and the earth-ground wire running off the
     tip to a grounded chassis. */}

The solenoid's strike/hold timing (a hit-and-hold sequence over the control board's spindle PWM
output) is a firmware and tuning concern, not a mechanical one — see [firmware](/en/hardware/firmware/)
and [tuning](/en/hardware/tuning/).

## Wiring (last)

Wiring comes last so nothing tugs while you position parts.

- **Components:** 1x MKS DLC32 V2.1 board (in its printed control-board holder) + 2x TMC2209
  drivers + 12 V 2 A barrel PSU + the 4K/8MP autofocus USB webcam.
- **There is soldering.** Splices are soldered: have a **soldering iron and a heat gun** (for
  heat-shrink) ready. Use **0.3 mm² lead wire** and spiral wrap for the looms.
- **Moving harness:** a ~400 mm length of PTFE/FEP tube routes the moving cable bundle — but the
  **wire runs outside the tube**, the tube only guides it.
- **Driver remap is a firmware matter.** The X driver maps to **Motor B**, the Y1 driver to
  **Motor A**, and the spindle output drives the **solenoid** — but you set this in firmware, not
  by rewiring. See [firmware](/en/hardware/firmware/).

     and USB plugged in, and the moving harness running through the PTFE tube to the gantry. */}

## After the last bolt

Assembly does not make the rig usable on its own. The next steps are a gate, in order:

<Steps>

1. **Flash the firmware first.** Load **FluidNC** onto the MKS DLC32 over USB (a browser flash).
   This is a **prerequisite gate** — `physiclaw setup hardware` will not work until the firmware is
   on. See [firmware](/en/hardware/firmware/).

2. **Tune the CoreXY.** Set belt tension to the target, finish re-greasing and aligning the rails,
   and tune the solenoid timing. Iterative and finicky — plan for it. See [tuning](/en/hardware/tuning/).

3. **Install the software and calibrate.** Then run the calibration wizard so the agent learns
   where the screen is. See [calibrate](/en/set-up/calibrate/).

</Steps>
