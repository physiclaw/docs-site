PhysiClaw's arm is a from-scratch, hand-built **CoreXY gantry** — extrusion, linear rails, GT2
belts, printed joints, and a solenoid-driven stylus on Z. Plan **about two to three hours** for
the assembly itself, inside a multi-day project: source and print parts, assemble, flash firmware,
then calibrate.

The complete, bolt-by-bolt instructions — every stage, every exploded-view figure — live in the
**build manual**. Open it below, grab the printable PDF, or download the 3D model to inspect the
assembled rig.

:::tip[No CAD app?]
The download is a `.step` file. To open it and spin the assembly around, use
[FreeCAD](https://www.freecad.org/) — free, open-source, and cross-platform.
:::

:::caution[Three steps that silently brick the rig]
The manual covers everything, but get these right or taps never land:

- **Earth-ground the stylus tip** to a verified earth (e.g. a grounded PC chassis) — never a wall
  socket, never the board GND, or the screen won't register a touch.
- **Loctite 243 the drive-pulley set screws** onto the shaft flat — loose set screws are the #1
  cause of silent position drift.
- **Equal A/B belt tension** (~75 Hz at the 220 mm carriage) — mismatched belts skew diagonal moves.
:::

Get the parts first on [Sourcing](/en/hardware/sourcing/); flash the
[Firmware](/en/hardware/firmware/) once the rig is built.
