PhysiClaw's X/Y motion is a hand-built **CoreXY gantry** you source as raw parts — there is no
ready-made arm. Parts run roughly **$145 USD** (about ¥1046 in China; higher in USD/EU markets),
spread across a long list, so the ordering *workflow* matters more than any single deal.

The full itemized **BOM**, the three buy / cut / print buckets, realistic cost, and the order to
place things in so the slow parts arrive in parallel all live in the **sourcing guide**. The 9
custom parts go to a 3D-print service as STEP files.

:::caution[Print in PA12 by SLS/MJF — not FDM]
The custom parts are load-bearing fit parts. Print them in **black PA12 nylon via powder-bed
fusion (SLS or MJF)**. FDM is rejected — the side holes deform and the overhangs sag.
:::

Once the parts are in hand, [Assembly](/en/hardware/assembly/) puts them together; then flash the
[Firmware](/en/hardware/firmware/).
