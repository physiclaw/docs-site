your expectations, and the rest of this section depends on it.

## Read this first: what you are actually building

If you came from OpenClaw expecting to bolt a stylus onto a cheap, ready-made pen plotter, reset
that picture now. **PhysiClaw is a from-scratch, hand-built CoreXY desktop gantry.** It is not a
kit, not a consumer plotter, and there is no single product you can buy and "convert." You source
raw aluminium extrusion, custom-cut and tap it, print nylon parts, and assemble the whole motion
system yourself.

The machine is three subsystems:

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="CoreXY stage" icon="rocket">
    A two-motor CoreXY gantry moves the stylus in X and Y across the phone screen. Two NEMA 17
    steppers stay fixed and drive a single belt path; the carriage rides on MGN9H linear rails.
    This is the precision heart of the build.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Touch mechanism (Z)" icon="pencil">
    The Z axis is a 12 V push-pull **solenoid**, not a servo and not a plotter pen. It snaps a
    capacitive stylus down to tap and releases to lift. Crisp, repeatable strikes are what let a
    tap read differently from a long-press.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Overhead camera" icon="laptop">
    A single 4K autofocus USB webcam looks straight down at the screen. It reads the screen and
    checks where the stylus is sitting before a tap. There is no second camera.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

:::caution[Manage your expectations before you spend money]
This is a multi-day project, not an afternoon. There is custom cutting and tapping, parts ordered
from print and supply vendors with multi-day lead times, a firmware flash, and a finicky
calibration/tuning phase. Read the whole build manual and check the complete BOM **before**
ordering anything — small mistakes in cut lengths or part counts are expensive to redo.
:::

## Cost and effort

### Cost

Parts come to roughly **$145 USD** total. Sourced in China that is about **¥1046** (at ~7.2
CNY/USD). In USD/EU markets the same parts cost **more** — treat the figures below as a
China-centric floor, not a fixed price.

The number is mostly a handful of line items:

| Group | Approx. (CNY) |
| --- | --- |
| 3D-printed nylon parts | ¥190 |
| Camera (4K autofocus webcam) | ¥200 |
| Linear rails + carriages | ¥120 |
| Control board (MKS DLC32 V2.1) | ¥100 |
| Aluminium extrusion | ¥60 |
| Steppers (2x NEMA 17) | ¥50 |
| Power supply (12 V 2 A) | ¥50 |
| TMC2209 drivers (x2) | ¥35 |

Fasteners and small hardware are not a single line item: screws, T-nuts, shims, and spacers are
sold in **20/50/100-piece packs**, so even though each piece is cheap, the "small parts" total
adds up. Budget for it.

### Effort: four phases

<Steps>

1. **Source, print, and cut.** Buy commodity parts, message vendors for **custom cut/tap/drill**
   work on the extrusion, and upload STEP files to a print service for the nylon parts. The supply
   chain is China-centric and lead times are measured in days. See
   [sourcing](/en/hardware/sourcing/) and [printed parts](/en/hardware/printed-parts/).

2. **Assemble.** The manual budgets **about two to three hours** of mechanical assembly once all
   parts are in hand. See [assembly](/en/hardware/assembly/).

3. **Flash firmware.** Load FluidNC onto the control board over USB (a browser flash). This is a
   prerequisite gate before software setup. See [firmware](/en/hardware/firmware/).

4. **Calibrate and tune.** Set CoreXY belt tension, re-grease and align the rails, and tune the
   solenoid. This phase is iterative and finicky — plan for it. See [tuning](/en/hardware/tuning/).

</Steps>

## The parts list

The authoritative source is the **13-section build manual** plus the **build123d procedure
modules** that generate the part geometry. This page is a planning summary — cross-check counts
and dimensions against the manual before ordering.

### Frame — aluminium extrusion (custom cut/tapped/drilled)

All extrusion is black-anodized and EU-profile. The lengths and the machining are specific; order
the cuts and taps from your vendor (see [sourcing](/en/hardware/sourcing/)).

| Part | Spec | Qty | Machining |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Side rails | 2040, 345 mm | 2 | Counterbored M6 |
| End rails | 2040, 190 mm | 2 | Tapped M6 |
| Gantry X-beam | 1020, 185 mm | 1 | — |
| Phone-bed supports | 1020, 230 mm | 2 | — |

### Linear motion

| Part | Spec | Qty |
| --- | --- | --- |
| MGN9H rail (Y) | 240 mm | 2 |
| MGN9H rail (X) | 150 mm | 1 |
| MGN9H H-type carriage | 4x M3, 16x15 mm mounting pitch | 3 |

:::note[Rails ship oiled]
MGN9H rails arrive coated in shipping oil. Wipe it off and re-grease with lithium grease before
use — this is part of the [tuning](/en/hardware/tuning/) phase.
:::

### Belt and pulleys

| Part | Spec | Qty |
| --- | --- | --- |
| GT2 belt, 6 mm | 1x 5 m roll, cut into equal A and B lengths | 1 roll |
| GT2 20T drive pulley | 5 mm bore | 2 |
| GT2 20T smooth idler | — | 10 |
| GT2 20T toothed idler | — | 2 |

That is **12 idlers total** (10 smooth + 2 toothed).

### Actuation

| Part | Spec | Qty |
| --- | --- | --- |
| NEMA 17 stepper | 42-step, for X/Y | 2 |
| Solenoid (Z/touch) | 12 V 0.8 A push-pull, 10 mm stroke, 0.2–6 N, 2x M3 mounts at 15 mm pitch | 1 |
| Capacitive stylus tip | M3 female thread, ~Ø7 mm conductive head | 1 |

The stylus tip threads onto the solenoid **core**, replacing the nut the solenoid ships with. This
is the Z mechanism — there is no servo and no pen.

### Electronics

| Part | Spec | Qty |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Control board | MKS DLC32 V2.1 (+ USB cable) | 1 |
| Stepper driver | TMC2209 | 2 |
| Power supply | 12 V 2 A, barrel jack | 1 |
| USB webcam | 4K / 8 MP, autofocus | 1 |

### Fasteners and hardware (~250+ pieces)

A from-scratch gantry needs a lot of small metal. Expect **over 250 individual pieces**, sold in
20/50/100-piece packs (which is why a "small parts" budget matters):

- **Screws:** BHCS / FHCS / SHCS in M3, M5, M6, plus 1/4-20.
- **Nuts:** drop-in / slide-in T-nuts and square nuts.
- **Hardware:** 10x shoulder screws, 12x shims, 8x spacers.
- **Brackets:** 6 flat brackets, 2 motor brackets, 1 corner bracket.
- **Feet:** 4 bumper feet.

### Consumables

| Item | Notes |
| --- | --- |
| Lithium grease | NLGI 2, for the rails |
| Loctite 243 | Thread-lock — see caution below |
| Heat-shrink | For wire splices |
| PTFE/FEP tube | ~400 mm; **wire runs outside the tube** |
| Spiral wrap | Cable management |
| Lead wire | 0.3 mm² |

:::caution[Loose set screws are the #1 reported failure]
Apply Loctite 243 to set screws — especially the drive-pulley set screws. Loose set screws are the
single most common problem builders report. Thread-lock is not optional here.
:::

### Tools

- 2 / 3 / 5 mm hex keys
- An electric driver (recommended — there are a lot of screws)
- A **soldering iron + heat gun** for wire splices — yes, this build involves soldering
- A flat reference surface to square the frame
- A phone tuning app for measuring belt tension

## 3D-printed parts

There are **9 unique designs / 11 pieces total**. The idler mount prints **x4** (2 front-mount + 2
motor-mount variants), plus: XY joint left, XY joint right, belt clamp, solenoid mount, phone bed,
control-board holder, and tube holder.

:::caution[Material and process are not negotiable]
Print in **black PA12 nylon via powder-bed fusion (SLS or MJF)**. **FDM is rejected** — the side
holes deform and overhangs sag, and the parts will not hold the motion system true. Order from a
print service using the provided STEP files. The geometry comes from the build123d Python parts,
exported to STEP and bundled as a downloadable zip — see [printed parts](/en/hardware/printed-parts/).
:::

## What actually matters

Not every part needs to be good. Spend your attention where it counts:

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Precision-critical" icon="warning">
    The **linear motion** (rails and carriages), **belt and pulley** quality, and a **crisp
    solenoid** drive on Z. These determine whether a tap lands where the agent aimed and whether a
    long-press reads differently from a tap. Buy good parts, grease and align carefully, and tune
    properly.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Forgiving" icon="approve">
    The **camera** (any 4K autofocus USB webcam is fine) and the **commodity fasteners**. These you
    can source on price. But do not extend this to the whole build — this is a precision machine,
    not a plug-together commodity kit.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>
