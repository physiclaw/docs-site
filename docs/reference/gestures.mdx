You already know the brain: an LLM calls an [MCP tool](/en/reference/mcp-tools/) like `tap` or
`swipe` with a **bbox** in `0`‚Äì`1` screen fractions. This page is the body ‚Äî what those tool
calls actually do to the rig. It is the **source of truth for solenoid timing**: every
constant below is taken straight from `solenoid.py` and `arm.py`, and where older pages
disagree, this page wins.

Every gesture is the same two-machine dance: the **X/Y arm** drives the stylus to the bbox
center ‚Äî via the calibrated `pct_to_grbl` affine that turns a percent coordinate into GRBL
millimetres ‚Äî then a **solenoid** drops the tip onto the glass and lifts it. The Z axis is
*not* a servo, and there's no depth to calibrate: the tip sits at a fixed mechanical height.
The only thing that varies between gestures is **timing** (and strike-vs-hold current). What
makes a tap a tap and a long-press a hold is how long the coil stays energized ‚Äî not how hard
or how deep it presses.

## The solenoid, in one paragraph

The stylus tip is pushed down by a **12V 0.8A push-pull solenoid** wired to the GRBL/FluidNC
**spindle PWM** pin (`gpio.32`), so it's driven with spindle G-code: `M3 S<duty>` energizes
the coil at a PWM duty on a `0`‚Äì`1000` scale, and `M5` releases it. A return spring lifts the
tip when the coil cuts. A solenoid burns its coil out if held at full current, so PhysiClaw
uses a **hit-and-hold** profile: strike hard to pull the iron core in, then drop to a lower
hold current to keep it seated.

## Canonical timing

| Constant | Value | What it is |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `HIT_S` | `1000` | strike duty ‚Äî pulls the core in (brief pull-in only) |
| `HOLD_S` | `750` | hold duty ‚Äî the only level safe to *sustain* (long-press, swipe slide) |
| `SETTLE_MS` | `80ms` | dwell after the strike before dropping to the hold level |
| `RELEASE_MS` | `200ms` | spring-rebound dwell after `M5`, before any XY move |
| `DOUBLE_TAP_GAP_MS` | `100ms` | brief lift between the two strikes of a double-tap |
| `TAP_DURATION` | `0.08s` | how long a tap stays on the glass |
| `LONG_PRESS_DURATION` | `1.2s` | how long a long-press stays seated |

:::caution[Why `HOLD_S` is below `HIT_S`]
The strike duty (`S1000`) is for the ~80ms pull-in only; sustaining it would cook the coil.
`HOLD_S` (`S750`) is the only level safe to hold continuously ‚Äî and it's deliberately *high*:
at a cooler `S500` the tip eased off at the strike‚Üíhold transition and the panel read a
long-press as a single tap. `S500` is fine for a *moving* swipe (the slide re-establishes
contact) but not for a still hold. The driver **refuses to construct** if `HOLD_S` isn't
strictly below `HIT_S` ‚Äî its `__init__` raises `ValueError`.
:::

After every `M5`, PhysiClaw dwells `RELEASE_MS` so the spring can lift the tip clear before
the next XY move starts ‚Äî otherwise the tip would drag a line across the screen.

## Tap

A momentary press: strike at `HIT_S`, hold for the **tap duration (80ms)**, release. The press
is short enough that the reduced hold current isn't needed ‚Äî `S1000` for 80ms gives a crisp
contact without cooking the coil.

```text title="Tap ‚Äî G-code"
G0 X.. Y..        ; arm moves to the bbox center
M3 S1000          ; strike (stylus down)
G4 P0.08          ; hold 80 ms
M5                ; release
G4 P0.2           ; spring-rebound dwell (tip clears the glass)
```

## Double-tap

Two strikes separated only by a brief contact-breaking lift (`DOUBLE_TAP_GAP_MS = 100ms`) ‚Äî
**not** the full 200ms spring-clear dwell. The whole pair runs as **one gesture** (a single
energized window) so down-to-down stays well under the iOS ~300ms double-tap window. Chaining
two independent taps (each with its own 200ms release) pushed it to ~280ms, and timing jitter
then split the gesture into two single taps.

```text title="Double-tap ‚Äî G-code"
M3 S1000          ; strike 1
G4 P0.08          ; hold 80 ms
M5                ; lift briefly‚Ä¶
G4 P0.1           ; ‚Ä¶100 ms, just enough to break contact
M3 S1000          ; strike 2
G4 P0.08          ; hold 80 ms
M5                ; release
G4 P0.2           ; spring-rebound dwell
```

## Long-press

Here the hit-and-hold profile earns its name: strike at `S1000`, settle 80ms, drop to the
`S750` hold current, and keep the tip seated for the full **long-press duration (1.2s)** before
lifting. iOS/Android register a long-press at ~500ms; 1.2s leaves comfortable margin.

```text title="Long-press ‚Äî G-code"
G0 X.. Y..        ; arm moves to the bbox center
M3 S1000          ; strike
G4 P0.08          ; settle 80 ms
M3 S750           ; drop to hold current
G4 P1.2           ; hold 1.2 s
M5                ; release
G4 P0.2           ; spring-rebound dwell
```

## Swipe

A swipe presses, then *moves under load*. The tip is held down at `HOLD_S` (`S750`) for the
whole slide while the arm runs a controlled `G1` **linear** move (not a rapid `G0`) from the
start to the endpoint, then lifts.

The `direction` is the **stylus** motion, not the page motion ‚Äî a swipe *up* scrolls the page
*down*. Stroke length comes from the `size` argument (`_SWIPE_DISTANCES`), which maps to a
fraction of the screen; the low-level arm default (`SWIPE_DISTANCE`) is 15mm.

| `size` | Screen fraction | Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `s` | `0.10` | small nudge |
| `m` | `0.30` | most scrolls |
| `l` | `0.50` | long scroll |
| `xl` | `0.75` | page-sized scroll |
| `xxl` | `0.90` | full-screen (Control / Notification Center) |

The `speed` argument sets the `G1` feed rate (`SWIPE_SPEEDS`):

| `speed` | Feed | Feel |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `slow` | `F3000` | careful scroll / drag |
| `medium` | `F6000` | normal swipe (~100 mm/s) |
| `fast` | `F10000` | fling, page switch |

```text title="Swipe ‚Äî G-code"
G0 X.. Y..        ; arm moves to the start
M3 S1000          ; strike
G4 P0.08          ; settle 80 ms
M3 S750           ; drop to hold current (tip stays down for the slide)
G1 X.. Y.. F6000  ; drag to the endpoint at the chosen feed
M5                ; release
G4 P0.2           ; spring-rebound dwell
```

:::note[Why the hold current, not the strike, drives the slide]
A stationary hold needs `S750` to stay registered, but a *moving* tip re-establishes contact
as it slides ‚Äî `S500` would be fine for a swipe yet not for a still hold. PhysiClaw uses the
one safe-to-sustain level (`S750`) for both, so there's a single hold path to reason about.
:::

## Summary

| Gesture | Down | Hold | Move | Up |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Tap | `S1000` | 80ms | ‚Äî | `M5` + 200ms dwell |
| Double-tap | `S1000` √ó2 | 80ms each, 100ms gap | ‚Äî | `M5` + 200ms dwell |
| Long-press | `S1000` ‚Üí `S750` | 1.2s | ‚Äî | `M5` + 200ms dwell |
| Swipe | `S1000` ‚Üí `S750` | for the slide | `G1` at `F3000/6000/10000` | `M5` + 200ms dwell |

:::tip[Safety nets]
Every gesture de-energizes the coil before returning ‚Äî even on an exception or Ctrl-C
(`try/finally` plus a `_force_off` / `_energized` context manager) ‚Äî so a fault mid-tap can
never leave the coil hot. As a hardware backstop, the FluidNC firmware (`off_on_alarm: true`)
drops the spindle PWM whenever the board enters an alarm state.
:::

## Tuning prerequisite

For any of this to work, the board must be in **spindle mode** (`$32=0`). In laser mode the
firmware emits spindle PWM only *while moving*, so a stationary `M3` strike produces nothing ‚Äî
the tap never lands. A buzzing coil at hold means the PWM is audible: fit a 470¬µF cap across
the coil or raise the PWM to 25kHz. The FluidNC config owns `$32`/`$30` from YAML and rejects
live `$`-writes (`error:162`), so the driver sends them with `optional=True` ‚Äî a bare GRBL
board (which *does* accept the writes) still works. Full procedure on the
[firmware page](/en/hardware/firmware/).
