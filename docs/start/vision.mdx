The age of the personal assistant has arrived.

Soon, an assistant will help with every part of your life. It will order your dinner and buy your groceries. It will book your doctor visits and clear your inbox. It will plan your trips, set up your holidays, and keep your calendar straight. It will work while you sleep. It will know you better than anyone — what you like, what you do each day, what you worry about. Always on, always there, every hour, just for you.

To build this, we need the right foundation.

And there is a real danger here. An assistant that helps with all of your life must see all of your life — everything you do, online and off. Day by day, it learns who you are. It will hold more about you than you could ever write down.

So ask one question: who owns all of that?

It cannot be big tech. If they own your assistant, they own you. Your data is not just handy — it is power. Whoever holds it can watch you. Shape you. Steer your choices. And turn on you the moment what you want gets in the way of what they want. Put that power in a few companies' hands, and it stops being a helper. It becomes a threat — to your freedom, and to the free and democratic society we all share.

When it comes to your whole life, there is only one person you can fully trust: you.

That is why we are building this on one simple rule. You are in control. Your data stays on your own computer. You give the orders. You own everything your assistant creates. Your life stays yours.

PhysiClaw is the first step.

It is early — rough, unfinished, far from perfect. But it works. Right now, it lifts real weight off your shoulders, doing the small jobs on your phone that used to eat your day. And it proves something bigger: another way is possible.

And it can't be only for a lucky few. So we build it in the open, and we give it away — both the robot and the software. Anyone can own one. The only cost is the parts to put it together, a price almost anyone can meet.

One assistant for each person. Owned by no giant. No one left behind because they were shut out. No one allowed to hold on to another person's data.

We are building a future where your personal assistant belongs to you, and to you alone — owned by you, run by you, out of reach of any company and any government.

That is how we stay free.
