---
title: Vision
description: Why PhysiClaw exists — building the foundation for a personal assistant you truly own.
---

The age of the personal assistant has arrived.

Soon, an assistant will help with every part of your life. It will order your dinner and buy your groceries. It will book your doctor visits and clear your inbox. It will plan your trips, arrange your holidays, and keep your calendar straight. It will work while you sleep. It will know you better than anyone alive — what you like, what you do each day, what you fear. Always on, always there, every hour, just for you.

But the same thing that makes it powerful makes it dangerous. To help with all of your life, it must see all of your life — everything you do, online and off. Day by day, it learns who you are, until it holds more about you than you could ever write down yourself.

So everything turns on one question: who owns all of that?

It cannot be big tech. If they own your assistant, they own you. Your data is not just useful — it is power. Whoever holds it can watch you, shape you, and steer your choices. They can turn on you the moment what you want stands in the way of what they want. Hand that power to a few companies, and your helper becomes your warden — a threat to your freedom, and to the free and democratic society we share.

So we build on one rule that never bends: your life belongs to you. Your data stays on your own computer. You give the orders. You own everything your assistant does and everything it learns. No company can see it. No government can take it.

PhysiClaw is our first step toward that. It is early — rough, unfinished, far from perfect. But it works. Today it already lifts real weight off your minds, handling the small tasks on your phone that used to swallow your hours. And it proves the harder point: another way is possible.

It cannot be a privilege for the few. So we build it in the open and give it away — the hardware and the software, both. Anyone can own one. The only price is the parts to build it, low enough for almost anyone to afford. One assistant for every person. Held by no monopoly. No one shut out, and no one left holding another person's data.

But even given away, the assistant is not yet wholly yours. The hardware sits in your hands. The software is yours to read and to change. Yet the mind it thinks with is not. Today, PhysiClaw runs on a large language model, and the strongest of these cannot fit on the computer in your home. To use it, your assistant must send your life to a server you do not own — so the rule we set, that no company sees your life, is not yet fully kept. This is the last place big tech still stands inside the loop.

To close that gap, we need a new kind of model — small enough to hold, strong enough to reason on its own. Not a vast store of everything ever written, but a sharp and simple core: the power to think, to plan, to act. We call it a world model. It lets go of what routine work never needs — the trivia, the endless memorized detail — and keeps only what matters: the ability to reason. Knowledge can be loaded when the moment asks for it. Reasoning stays at the center, always. Strip away the rest, and the model shrinks — small enough to own, small enough for everyone.

Today's large models are the computers of the 1960s — room-sized, costly, locked away, owned by the few. We believe they will travel the road the computer did: from the mainframe to the personal machine, from the few to everyone. Smaller. Cheaper. Yours. Stronger each year, with all the world's knowledge a reach away.

And this, too, is more than convenience. It is power. Just as data in a few hands is a tool of control, so is the mind. Democracy rests on the strength of ordinary people, and a free society holds only when the many can answer the few. A mind owned by everyone is that answer. Concentrated, it rules us. Shared, it keeps us free.

When that day comes, the loop is whole. Everything it learns, everything it does, and the mind it does it with — all yours. Owned by you. Answering to you. Held by no one else.

This is the future we are building: a personal assistant that answers to you and no one else — owned by you, within reach of everyone, beyond the grasp of any company or government.

That is how we keep our lives our own. That is how we stay free.
